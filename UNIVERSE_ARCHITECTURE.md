# Universe Architecture

## Book of Existence

This document defines the fundamental architecture, laws, and concepts of the Universe system.

---

# Chapter I ďż˝ Existence

Existence is the highest-level domain of the Universe system.

It defines the conditions under which anything may:

- become possible,
- enter reality,
- develop potential,
- become actualized,
- and be preserved in history.

Existence does not decide what will happen.

It defines what it means for something to be able to happen.

# Chapter II ďż˝ Reality

Reality is the active state of existence.

It contains the processes, structures, and conditions that currently operate within a universe.

Reality does not choose outcomes.

It provides the environment from which potentials can emerge.

Reality may contain:

- natural processes,
- active structures,
- environmental conditions,
- and sources of potential.

# Chapter III ďż˝ Possibility

Possibility represents everything that could exist before reality selects from it.

A possibility has no influence on reality until it becomes a potential.

Possibility does not act, choose, or enter history.

It is only the space of what may become available.

When reality makes a possibility available for actualization, it becomes a potential.

# Chapter IV ďż˝ Potential

Potential is a possibility that has become available within reality.

It can participate in an actualization cycle.

It can participate in an actualization cycle.

A potential may be:

- open,
- actualized,
- or unrealized.

An open potential is waiting for resolution.

An actualized potential became part of reality and history.

An unrealized potential was available but was not selected.

# Chapter V - Actualization

Actualization is the process by which open potentials are resolved.

It determines which potentials become actualized and which remain unrealized.

The ActualizationCycle contains the available potentials.

The Actualizer selects and resolves them.

The result of every actualization cycle is permanently recorded in History.


# Chapter VI - History

History is the permanent record of resolved actualization cycles.

It preserves what became actualized and what remained unrealized.

History does not overwrite the past.

Alternative outcomes are preserved as separate branches.

Each history record belongs to exactly one branch.

A record stores its branch identifier, parent branch, and the observer that created it.

History prevents duplicate records within the same branch and cycle.
